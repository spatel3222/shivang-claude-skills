# GTM Playbooks

## Pre-Playbook: Validation Before Marketing

Before spending a single hour on marketing, validate the problem:
- Talk to 100+ potential users in your target market. Not surveys — conversations.
- Identify the unifying pain point. If people are building workarounds, that's a strong signal.
- Build the smallest experiment that proves people will pay.
- The only advantage you have over an incumbent is urgency and speed. Launch imperfect V1s. Earlier you launch, the faster results compound and the faster you learn whether to scale, pivot, or kill.
- Most companies that fail die from internal combustion (bad decisions, slow execution), not competition. The market is big enough — stop obsessing over competitors and start building.

## The First 100 Customers Playbook

Based on proven SaaS GTM strategies (validated by companies scaling to $3M+ ARR). Three phases, executed in order but overlapping.

### Phase 1: Capture the Low-Hanging Fruit (Week 1-4)

Go after people already searching for a solution. Don't create demand — capture existing demand.

#### Tactic 1A: Bottom-of-Funnel SEO — Competitor Comparison Pages
- Create "[Your Product] vs [Every Competitor]" pages
- Target EVERY competitor you can find, not just the big ones
- Imperfect V1 pages that are LIVE beat perfect pages that aren't
- These pages get cited by LLMs (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) — optimize for that
- Monitor which comparison pages get traction → double down and optimize those
- **Why it works:** People searching "[Competitor] alternative" are highest-intent buyers. They already know they need a solution.
- **Tool stack:** Ahrefs/SEMrush for keyword research, your CMS for publishing, Supademo for embedded interactive demos on each page

#### Tactic 1B: Mid-Funnel — Free Tools
- Build ungated free tools in adjacent problem spaces
- Let people access parts of your product without signing up
- Examples: free screenshot tool, free tutorial creator, free calculator, free template
- Key: NO gate. No signup required. Let people find value first.
- **Why it works:** People discover you while solving an adjacent problem. 15-20% visitor-to-signup conversion is achievable. These tools can drive 20% of all traffic long-term.
- **Tool stack:** Your product (carve out free features), Supademo for interactive walkthroughs

#### Tactic 1C: Top-of-Funnel — Programmatic SEO + LLM Optimization
- Create step-by-step interactive content for thousands of workflow keywords
- "How to [action] in [tool]" — with your product embedded as the solution
- Programmatic pages at scale, each with an interactive demo
- Optimize for both Google AND LLM citation
- **Why it works:** Solves the user's question in an engaging way. Your product is front and center. Even if only a fraction are in your ICP, the volume compounds.
- **Tool stack:** CMS with programmatic page generation, Supademo for embedded demos, Ahrefs for keyword targeting

### Phase 2: Hand-to-Hand Combat (Week 2-8)

Do things that don't scale. Personally engage with potential users one by one.

#### Tactic 2A: Community Value-First Outreach
- Go to Reddit, Indie Hackers, niche Slack groups, Discord servers, Facebook groups
- DON'T spam your product. Instead, offer genuine value.
- Example: "Post your product URL and I'll build [something useful] for you for free"
- Do this publicly — other people watching will see the value and either ask for help or sign up themselves
- **Why it works:** Public generosity creates social proof. One comment thread can generate 10+ signups. People who receive free value become advocates.

#### Tactic 2B: Reddit/Community Deep Engagement
- Find every subreddit where your ICP hangs out
- Answer questions genuinely. Become a known helpful person.
- When relevant (and ONLY when relevant), mention your product as a solution
- Create value posts — "I analyzed X and here's what I found"
- **Compliance:** Reddit hates self-promotion. 90% value, 10% mention. If your post history is all product plugs, you'll get banned.

#### Tactic 2C: Direct Founder Outreach
- Reach out directly to founders/users who could benefit
- Offer to set something up FOR them — remove all friction
- Don't pitch. Help. The pitch is implicit in the help.
- **Tool stack:** Apollo for finding contacts, LinkedIn for warm outreach

### Phase 3: Distribution Density (Week 4+)

Be everywhere your users are. No single magic channel — compound across many.

#### Tactic 3A: Building in Public
- Share metrics, learnings, failures, and wins on LinkedIn and X
- Be transparent about revenue, growth, and challenges
- **Why it works:** Founders and decision-makers follow other builders. Transparency builds trust and creates organic distribution.
- **Content types to create:** Weekly metric updates, lessons learned posts, behind-the-scenes of product decisions, customer win stories (with permission)

#### Tactic 3B: SEO + LLM Engine (Ongoing)
- Continue expanding comparison pages, free tools, and programmatic content
- Monitor which pages LLMs cite — optimize those
- Target: 30-40% of traffic from SEO + LLM over time

#### Tactic 3C: Viral Loops + Word of Mouth
- Build shareability into the product (watermarks, share buttons, "made with" badges)
- Referral programs (even informal ones)
- Target: 30% of traffic from word of mouth over time

#### Tactic 3D: Cold Outreach at Scale
- Layer in cold email and LinkedIn outreach
- Only after you have proof points (users, testimonials, case studies)
- **Why wait:** Cold outreach without social proof has low conversion. With 50+ users and a few testimonials, your reply rates 3-5x.
- **Tool stack:** Apollo for prospecting + sequences, Instantly for high-volume delivery, Clay for enrichment

## The Content Waterfall System

The biggest content mistake: creating new content for every platform from scratch. The Content Waterfall turns 1 pillar piece into 20+ pieces across platforms with minimal incremental work.

### How It Works

1. **Create one pillar piece** — A deep LinkedIn post, YouTube video, or newsletter. Spend real time on this one idea.
2. **Repurpose down the waterfall:**
   - 1 LinkedIn post → 3 Instagram carousels → 3 X threads → 3 TikTok/Reels → 3 YouTube Shorts → Newsletter → Re-post on LinkedIn 3 months later
   - 1 YouTube video → clip into 3 Instagram Reels + 3 TikTok + 3 YouTube Shorts + community post
3. **Use templates that never change** — Same carousel format, same thread format, same reel style. Only swap the content/idea. This makes it easy for you AND your team.
4. **4 hours/week is enough:**
   - Hour 1: Plan 20 hooks (headlines/openers)
   - Hours 2-3: Fill in content for the pillar pieces
   - Hour 4: Set up CTAs + lead magnets for each piece

### Why This Matters for Strategy
When recommending content strategy, always recommend the waterfall approach — never tell someone to create unique content for each platform. The system multiplies output without multiplying effort. One strong idea, properly repurposed, can generate 10x the views of the original.

### Customer Research → Content Ideas
Survey existing customers with 3 questions:
1. What's your biggest goal this year?
2. What's your biggest challenge?
3. If I could wave a magic wand, what problem would you want solved?

Feed responses into AI (ChatGPT/Claude) → generate 100+ content ideas in "how to" and "how I" formats that solve their actual problems. Content that solves real customer problems converts naturally.

## The Lead Magnet Engine

Lead magnets are the bridge between free content and revenue. Simple formats (Google Docs, Google Slides) outperform fancy PDFs because they feel more authentic and are easier to create.

### The System
1. **Every piece of content ends with a lead magnet CTA** — "Comment [KEYWORD] to get [resource]"
2. **ManyChat automation triggers on the keyword** → DMs the user → captures email + phone → delivers lead magnet → tags lead in CRM (HubSpot)
3. **Tag leads by source content** — Know which topics/posts drive which leads. This tells you what content to double down on.
4. **Two types of lead magnets:**
   - **Value magnets** — Playbooks, scorecards, frameworks, templates. These help people and build trust.
   - **Visibility magnets** — Email courses (7-day, 21-day). These keep you top-of-mind over time and warm leads for conversion.

### Revenue Attribution
The full chain: Content → Comment trigger → ManyChat DM → Email/Phone capture → CRM tag → Newsletter → Sales funnel → Revenue. Every link in this chain should be tracked so you know which content actually makes money.

At scale, lead magnets can drive 80% of revenue. This sounds counterintuitive — free resources driving paid conversions — but the generosity builds trust that converts over time.

## Content-to-Revenue Metrics

Most people track vanity metrics (likes, follows, impressions). These three metrics actually correlate with revenue:

### 1. Email Signups Per Post
Track which posts drive the most email captures via ManyChat + HubSpot. Your content should convert to your owned audience (newsletter) because you don't own Instagram/LinkedIn/X. Each post should drive people to that list.

### 2. Revenue Per Follower
Calculate: Monthly cash collection from platform ÷ follower count. Track this monthly. If it's declining, your audience is growing but not converting — content/offer misalignment.

### 3. Platform Conversion Rate
Of the traffic you drive from a platform to your website/landing page, what % becomes customers? Compare month-over-month. If you changed content strategy in August, compare June vs. August conversion rates to see if the new strategy is more efficient with traffic.

**When recommending metrics to track, always include these three alongside channel-specific metrics.**

## Building the Marketing Team

The fastest growing accounts aren't run by the hardest-working founders — they're run by the smartest-hiring founders. This is about moving from creator to CEO of a personal media company.

### Hiring Order (Right Seats, Right Order)

| Hire | Role | Cost Range | When to Hire |
|------|------|-----------|--------------|
| 1. Social Media Manager | Head of distribution — right content, right format, right time, right caption | $3-7K/mo | When you're spending 20+ hrs/week on content and it's pulling you from revenue-generating work |
| 2. Designer | Visual consistency — grid, carousels, brand aesthetic | ~$3K/mo | When your content strategy is proven but visuals are inconsistent |
| 3. Video Editor | Reels, shorts, video cuts from long-form | $3-5K/mo | When video is a proven channel but editing is your bottleneck |

Total: $10-12K/mo for a full content team.

### The Delegation Math (ROI Calculator)

Before any hire, run this calculation:
1. **Cost of hire:** Monthly salary (e.g., $7K/mo)
2. **Hours saved per month:** Time they free up for you (e.g., 80 hrs/mo)
3. **Your revenue per hour:** What you generate when focused on sales/strategy (e.g., $2K/hr)
4. **Freed revenue:** Hours saved × your hourly rate × profit margin
5. **Net profit:** Freed revenue − cost of hire

Example: $7K hire saves 80 hrs → 80 × $2K = $160K revenue → at 50% margin = $80K profit → $80K − $7K = $73K net = 10.4x ROI.

**If a hire brings in even 1 incremental customer per month worth $10K, they pay for themselves.** The real cost isn't the salary — it's the opportunity cost of NOT hiring.

### When to Recommend Team Building
- User is spending 20+ hrs/week on content → recommend SMM
- Content strategy is working but visuals are inconsistent → recommend designer
- Video is proven but editing is the bottleneck → recommend editor
- User is doing everything themselves → show them the delegation math first, then let them decide

## Channel Mix Benchmarks (Mature SaaS)

| Source | % of Traffic | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| SEO + LLM | 30-40% | Compounds over time. Slow start, massive long-term payoff. |
| Word of Mouth / Viral | ~30% | Product-driven. Build shareability in. |

## Common Mistakes at Each Stage

### Pre-100 Users
- Chasing perfection instead of launching — ship imperfect V1, iterate with data
- Spending money on paid ads before proving organic traction
- Building in stealth for too long — launch yesterday
- Only doing one channel — density matters
- Automating too early — do it manually first, then automate what works

### 100-1000 Users
- Abandoning what got you to 100 (usually manual/community work)
- Over-investing in paid before optimizing organic conversion
- Not talking to users enough — you still need customer feedback loops
- Hiring marketers before you understand your own funnel

### 1000+ Users
- Not systemizing what works — if it's in one person's head, it doesn't scale
- Ignoring retention for acquisition — cheaper to keep users than find new ones
- Over-tooling — 50 marketing tools ≠ better marketing
